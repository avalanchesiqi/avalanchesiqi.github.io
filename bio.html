I am an Assistant Professor of Information and Library Science in the Luddy School of Informatics, Computing, and Engineering at Indiana University Bloomington. Previously, I was a postdoc research fellow in the Center for Social Media Responsibility at the University of Michigan School of Information. I obtained my Ph.D. (Computer Science) from the Australian National University, M.S. (Information Technology) from the University of Melbourne, and B.E. (Electronics Engineering) from Tianjin University.

I am a computational social scientist who collects, models, and analyzes web data at scale. My research aims to (a) advance our understanding of contemporary social phenomena via large-scale measurements and (b) design next generation of sociotechnical systems via policies and interventions driven by data. My previous work includes:

  • How to estimate the prevalence of each class in unlabeled datasets using black box classifiers? (ICWSM '24)
  • How do Twitter users from different countries consume news during the COVID-19 pandemic? (CSCW '23)
  • How does online attention transcend across platforms, across topics, and over time? (ICWSM '22)
  • How do liberals and conservatives engage in public discussions on YouTube? (ICWSM '21 )
  • How does Twitter API's sampling mechanism impact common measurements? (ICWSM '20)
  • How do YouTube recommendation networks drive user attention? (CSCW '19 , WSDM '21)
  • How much time do users spend on watching YouTube videos after clicking? (ICWSM '18)

I am an avid long-distance runner – 2:42 marathoner, winner of ANU Inward Bound '17 Division 1, and finisher of 70+ ultra marathons.
