I am a postdoc research fellow in the Center for Social Media Responsibility at the University of Michigan, working with Prof. Paul Resnick. I obtained my Ph.D. (Computer Science) from the Australian National University, M.S. (Information Technology) from the University of Melbourne, and B.E. (Electronics Engineering) from Tianjin University. My Ph.D. advisor is Prof. Lexing Xie.

I am a computational social scientist who collects, models, and analyzes web data at scale. I produce descriptive measurement results on topics such as content consumption and political polarization, and contribute a variety of resources that aid in these measurements. My previous work includes:

  • How to measure the prevalence of each class in unlabeled datasets using black box classifiers? (ICWSM '24)
  • How do Twitter users from different countries consume news during the COVID-19 pandemic? (CSCW '23)
  • How does online attention transcend across platforms, across topics, and over time? (ICWSM '22)
  • How do liberals and conservatives engage in public discussions on YouTube? (ICWSM '21 )
  • How does Twitter API's sampling mechanism impact common measurements? (ICWSM '20)
  • How does YouTube recommender system drive user attention? (CSCW '19 , WSDM '21)
  • How do YouTube users watch videos after clicking? (ICWSM '18)
