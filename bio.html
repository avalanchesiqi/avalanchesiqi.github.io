I am a postdoc research fellow in the CSMR Lab at the University of Michigan, working with Prof. Paul Resnick. Prior to UMSI, I was a research fellow in the Computational Media Lab at the Australian National University. I obtained my Ph.D. (Computer Science) from the Australian National University, M.S. (Information Technology) from the University of Melbourne, and B.E. (Electronics Engineering) from Tianjin University. My Ph.D. advisors are Prof. Lexing Xie and Dr. Marian-Andrei Rizoiu.

I am broadly interested in measuring and modeling collective human behavior on the web and social media. My work lies in the fields of computational social science, social computing, and data mining. Some previous work includes:

  • How does online attention transcend across platforms, across topics, and over time? (ICWSM'22)
  • How do liberals and conservatives engage in cross-partisan discussions on YouTube? (ICWSM'21)
  • How does Twitter API's sampling mechanism impact common measurements? (ICWSM'20)
  • How does YouTube recommender system drive user attention? (CSCW'19, WSDM'21)
  • Why do some YouTube videos keep the users staying longer? (ICWSM'18)

I am an avid long-distance runner – winner of ANU Inward Bound '17 Division 1, finisher of 18 100+km races and 50+ ultra marathons.
